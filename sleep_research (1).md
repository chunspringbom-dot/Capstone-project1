# 💤 The Impact of Sleep Environment on Sleep Quality among DICS Students

## 👤 Researcher

-   10th Grade, Chan-Young Kang

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 1. 🧠 Background

This project began with a simple question:\
"Why do students in our school fall asleep so often during class?"

In many other high schools, students often get less sleep (around 5
hours) but still manage to stay focused.\
However, students at our school usually get around 8 hours of sleep, yet
still feel sleepy during class.

This led to the idea that **sleep quality**, rather than just sleep
duration, might be an important factor.

Additionally, I became curious about whether the sleep-related
information presented in news and media is actually reliable.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 2. 🎯 Purpose

The goals of this study are:

-   To analyze how environmental factors affect sleep quality\
-   To identify conditions that improve sleep quality even with the same
    sleep duration\
-   To explore ways to reduce sleepiness during class and improve
    overall quality of life

In the long term, this project aims to develop a **personalized sleep
analysis system or app**.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 3. ❓ Research Questions

-   Does light affect sleep satisfaction?\
-   Does noise affect awakenings during sleep?\
-   What sleep duration is most effective?\
-   Do pre-sleep behaviors affect sleep quality?

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 4. 🔬 Hypotheses

1.  Dark and quiet environments improve sleep quality\
2.  Light reduces sleep quality\
3.  Noise negatively affects sleep\
4.  8 hours is optimal\
5.  Eating before sleep reduces sleep quality

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 5. 🧪 Methodology

### Variables

-   Light
-   Noise
-   Exercise
-   Food intake
-   Sleep duration

### Sleep Quality Indicators

-   Time to fall asleep
-   Wake-ups
-   Naps
-   Satisfaction
-   Wake condition

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 6. 📊 Results Summary

-   Quiet environments → higher sleep scores\
-   Dim light sometimes better than darkness\
-   Heavy snacks → unexpectedly higher scores\
-   Some short sleep cases → high scores
